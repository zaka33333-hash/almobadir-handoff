# Topic: Arabic finance + business media landscape — competitive map

> Wave 3 / Topical deep-dive 2 of 10 | Almobadir CONTENT Intelligence audit
> Date: 2026-04-27 | Sources: Wikipedia, SimilarWeb, HypeStat, Semrush, HypeAuditor, Favikon, IGYGrow, official sites, SRMG/Bloomberg/Argaam press releases, Wamda, Arab News, Entrepreneur Middle East, podcast feedspot rankings

## Executive summary (200 words)

Arabic business + finance media is dominated by two clusters that almost never compete head-to-head: (1) institutional publishers — SRMG-owned Argaam, Asharq News with Bloomberg, Al Eqtisadiah, Maaal, plus pan-regional independents Wamda, Forbes Middle East, Arabian Business, Mubasher, Magnitt, and Entrepreneur Al Arabiya — and (2) creator-led media that lives almost entirely on social platforms (Mashhoor Aldubayan, Faris Alturki, Naif Madkhali, Adwa Aldakheel, Thmanyah's Fnjan and Swalif Business, plus a long tail of TikTok/Instagram personal-finance accounts).

Almobadir, with ~3.3M reach across 7 IG + 4 TikTok handles in the "money + business + mindset" category, sits in the second cluster but punches at Tier-1 publisher reach when measured by audience attention, not pageviews. SimilarWeb shows that even Argaam — the #1 Arabic financial news site — pulls 5.2M monthly visits, while Asharq.com pulls 4.9M and Mubasher pulls "more than 2.5M." Almobadir's owned audience compares favourably on a per-month-attention basis, but cannot yet match institutional brand authority, advertiser trust, or repeat-visit web traffic.

The white space is clear: a media-tech operator producing entertainment-first, education-grade Arabic business content with a credible web destination + newsletter — a position no Tier-1 publisher and no individual creator currently holds.

## Tier 1 — established publishers (10 brands compared)

### 1. Al Eqtisadiah (الاقتصادية)

- **Country / origin:** Saudi Arabia (Riyadh) [1]
- **Founded:** 12 January 1992 by Hisham Hafiz and Mohammad Hafiz; published under Saudi Research and Publishing Company (SRMG group) [1]
- **Primary digital surface:** aleqt.com
- **Monthly traffic:** SimilarWeb reports ~322.4K total visits over the last three months (so roughly **107K/month**); global rank #178,549; Saudi rank #2,308; #934 among Saudi news/media publishers [2]
- **Top countries:** Saudi Arabia 39%, Egypt 13%, UAE 5%, Jordan 3%, US 3% [2]
- **Editorial angle:** Daily print + digital, exclusive economics/business/financial-markets focus, listed-company analysis
- **Positioning line:** "Saudi Arabia's veteran print-first business daily — slow-moving, authoritative, market-data heavy."

### 2. Argaam (أرقام)

- **Country / origin:** Saudi Arabia (Riyadh) [3]
- **Founded:** 1 March 2007 by Argaam Investment Company; SRMG acquired 51% in October 2017 for SAR 37.5M / ~US $10M [3][4]
- **Primary digital surface:** argaam.com
- **Monthly traffic:** **5.2M** total visits over the last three months (~1.7M/month); global rank #12,639; Saudi rank #801; #61 among Saudi news/media publishers [5]
- **Top countries:** Saudi Arabia 80.6%, Egypt 4.5%, US 3.5%, UAE 3.1% [5]
- **Audience:** 70% male, dominant 25–34 age band [5]
- **Engagement:** 3.6 pages/visit, 8 min avg session — exceptional retention for a news site
- **Editorial angle:** Tadawul (TASI) real-time market data, listed-company news, IPO/results coverage, region-wide stock data feed
- **Positioning line:** "The Bloomberg Terminal of Arabic — the must-open tab for anyone trading Saudi/GCC equities."

### 3. Mubasher (مباشر)

- **Country / origin:** UAE-incorporated, ops hub Cairo, offices in Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, UAE [6]
- **Founded:** 1990s (parent group long-established); .info portal dominant in 2000s-on
- **Primary digital surface:** mubasher.info
- **Monthly traffic:** **>2.5M monthly visitors** (their own figure) covering 14 markets; SimilarWeb confirms top traffic source is organic search 48.3%, direct 46.9% [6][7]
- **Top countries:** Egypt is the #1 traffic source [7]
- **Audience:** 77% male, 25–34 dominant [7]
- **Editorial angle:** Equity market news, data, charts, investor tools across 14 MENA markets
- **Positioning line:** "Pan-Arab retail-investor terminal — Egypt-led, Gulf-served."

### 4. AMEinfo

- **Country / origin:** UAE (Abu Dhabi → Dubai) [8]

- **Historical traffic:** 2.4M monthly unique browsers (March 2012, ABC audit); 2.2M readers across 150 countries (June 2016) [8]

Direct evidence: InboxReads' "Latest Arabic Substack newsletters" page lists only 4 — *Nashrat Maher* (jobs), *The Yoga Letters*, *Radeeff Community* (creator-economy), *Heads or Tails* (design) — none of them business/finance focused [37][38]. ### Tier 3 summary — the structural gap

There is **no dominant, modern, Saudi-Arabic, business-focused, Substack/Beehiiv-native creator newsletter** in the segment. Thmanyah is the closest analog but uses a native platform and is general-Arabic content (not business-only). Almobadir has *unusual room* to launch the category-defining "Saudi-Arabic business newsletter" — there is no entrenched incumbent in the niche.

## White-space gaps Almobadir could own (5 specific niches with evidence)

### Gap 1 — "Saudi-Arabic business newsletter" (zero incumbent)

**Evidence:** InboxReads' Arabic Substack feature lists 4 newsletters, none business [37][38]. The closest analog (Thmanyah) is general-Arabic culture content on a native platform. No Substack/Beehiiv-native, business-only Arabic newsletter has reached "incumbent" status. Asharq + Khaleej Times are *publisher* newsletters — no creator-led peer exists.

**Why Almobadir wins:** With 3.3M social followers ready to be funnelled into email, Almobadir could ship Saudi-Arabia's first creator-led business newsletter and own that label. Thmanyah is preoccupied as a general-content media-tech B-Corp; the niche is open.

### Gap 2 — "Money education for the working middle class" (the price-of-bread bracket)

**Evidence:** Tier-1 publishers serve professional investors (Argaam, Asharq, Mubasher) or executives (Forbes, Maaal). Tier-2 creators serve entrepreneurs/founders (Mashhoor, Faris, Naif). **No major brand serves a household-finance audience with monthly-budget-and-emergency-fund-grade education.** Adwa Aldakheel and Reyali Program (SEDCO Holding's CSR initiative) [43] touch this, but neither is a mass-market always-on media voice.

**Why Almobadir wins:** Almobadir's positioning ("when entertainment meets education") and presence on TikTok (the platform with the most working-class Arabic audience growth) is exactly suited. The 11-handle architecture lets one of the satellite handles (e.g., @almobadir_flousak) own this lane explicitly.

### Gap 3 — "Halal-aware modern business + investing"

**Evidence:** Abu Lahya (authority score 7,998 per Favikon) is the only meaningful Saudi creator anchored on halal-business framing [25]. None of the Tier-1 publishers do this seriously — Argaam reports markets, Maaal reports companies, Asharq reports macro. There is no "halal-aware modern personal-finance" media brand at scale.

**Why Almobadir wins:** The "mindset" pillar (already an existing handle theme) plus a halal-finance editorial line would convert religious differentiation into editorial differentiation. Aligns with Saudi Vision 2030 cultural framing without being a CSR initiative.

### Gap 4 — "EN bridge for diaspora + investors-into-MENA"

**Evidence:** Wamda's traffic geography (UAE 7.4%, US 6.4%, Saudi 6.3%, Nigeria 6.2%, India 4.7%) shows huge diaspora + foreign-investor demand [20]. AMEinfo's death in 2023 [8] vacated an English MENA-business aggregator role. Almobadir's planned EN mirror at /en/ can plug into this exact unmet demand — *but only if it's not a one-to-one translation of AR content; it must be optimised for the diaspora reader's questions.*

**Why Almobadir wins:** Almobadir is one of very few Arabic-first creator brands that even has plans for a serious EN mirror. Most Tier-2 creators don't. This is a *moat* if executed well.

### Gap 5 — "Video-first Arabic financial news under 2 minutes"

**Evidence:** TikTok's Arabic content has grown 300%+ since 2023 [25]. None of the Tier-1 publishers (Argaam, Asharq, Maaal, Forbes) have meaningful TikTok presence — they are TV/web operations. Mashhoor's Swalif Business is long-form (60+ min). @riyadh_today is news-ticker, not explainer. No one is doing the **"Arabic Cleo Abram" or "Arabic Morning Brew Daily"** — a 60–120 second daily video explainer of one financial concept.

**Why Almobadir wins:** Almobadir already has 4 TikTok handles. Centralising one of them on a daily explainer format would let it own the format the way Cleo Abram owns it in English — and there is no entrenched competitor.

## 5 specific website + content moves

### Move 1 — Launch the newsletter (and own the category before Thmanyah does)

Set up Beehiiv (creator-monetisable, no platform cut on subscriptions) as the platform of record. Brand it distinctly from the social handles — e.g., "نشرة المبادر" or "Almobadir Weekly." First six months: free weekly. Goal: get to 25K subscribers by month 6, leveraging the 3.3M social funnel. Beehiiv is recommended over Substack because Substack takes 10% and Beehiiv takes 0% [45]. **Evidence this is the right move:** Tier-3 finding — there is no incumbent Saudi-Arabic business newsletter with creator branding.

### Move 2 — Launch the EN mirror with a *different* editorial product, not a translation

The EN mirror at /en/ should not duplicate Arabic posts in English. Instead, it should serve the diaspora + foreign-investor segments visible in Wamda's traffic geography (UAE/US/Saudi/Nigeria/India). Sample EN-only formats: "How a Saudi household actually budgets," "Why Vision 2030 changed Arabic personal finance," "The Halal index: what's actually halal investing in 2026." Optimise for AEO (the Bencium AEO skill applies here) so ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini surface Almobadir when diaspora ask MENA business questions.

### Move 3 — Anchor a TikTok handle on "1 concept, 90 seconds, daily"

Tier-1 publishers have no TikTok play. Mashhoor is long-form. @riyadh_today is news-ticker. There is no Arabic equivalent of Cleo Abram (en.cleo.abram) — a daily, beautifully-designed 60–120 second explainer. Pick one of Almobadir's existing 4 TikTok handles, dedicate it explicitly to this format, and ship 5 a week. White-space win is highest here.

### Move 4 — Commission a "Halal Money 101" pillar

Abu Lahya is the only credible halal-aware business creator in the Top-20 Favikon list [25]. The category is a four-lane open road: halal investing, halal employment ethics, halal household finance, halal business law. Build a content pillar (10 articles + 10 videos) and stake the territory. Especially load-bearing because Saudi audiences disproportionately value this framing and no one is serving it well.

### Move 5 — Publish the "Almobadir 100" annual list to compete with Forbes ME's lists

Forbes Middle East owns the lists franchise [15]. But Forbes ME's lists are predictable (Most Powerful CEO, Richest Family). Almobadir can publish a different list — "Almobadir 100: the Arabs under 35 who'll change Arab money in the next decade" — and own the next generation of authority. Lists drive PR, partnerships, and inbound media coverage; they're how Forbes built its Arab moat in the first place.
