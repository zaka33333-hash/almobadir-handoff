## Executive summary

Saudi Vision 2030 enters its third and final phase in 2026 with 93% of performance indicators meeting or near targets — 309 of 390 active KPIs delivered, 935 of 1,290 initiatives completed, real GDP up 4.5% in 2025 (the strongest in three years), and the economy crossing the $1T mark. But the Vision is also visibly recalibrating: NEOM/The Line construction is suspended with the population target slashed from 1.5M to 300K, PIF has cut tens of billions in construction commitments, tourism funding has been scrapped, and capital is rotating toward Expo 2030, World Cup 2034, AI infrastructure, and Tadawul IPO depth. For Almobadir's audience — Arabic-speaking initiators, founders, finance-curious millennials/Gen Z — Vision 2030 is the dominant business narrative of 2026, but Arabic business media (Al Eqtisadiah, Argaam, Mubasher, Asharq, Mal) treats it as a press-release beat: official numbers, ministry statements, ribbon-cuttings. There is a wide-open lane for a creator-economy-native voice that does post-mortems, decodes regulatory shifts (Saudization 2026, ZATCA waves 23/24, foreign property law January 2026, open-banking licensing March 2026), runs Tadawul IPO previews/post-mortems, and interrogates the gap between SME contribution to GDP (22.9% vs. 35% target) and the 1.7M-strong SME base. Almobadir's network already has the audience, the founder voice, and the multi-handle architecture (mindset, business, flousak, media) to occupy this lane. The website is the missing surface — currently silent on the single biggest topic its audience cares about.

## Current pillars + 2026 status

### The three pillars (per vision2030.gov.sa)

The official Vision 2030 architecture has **three pillars** plus 13 Vision Realization Programs (VRPs). Most external coverage incorrectly cites four pillars by elevating "Quality of Life" to pillar status — but that's a VRP that rolls under Vibrant Society. Worth getting right when you publish.

1. **A Vibrant Society** — heritage, entertainment, sports, Hajj/Umrah, livable cities. Houses the **Quality of Life Program** VRP. 2026 anchors: Diriyah Gate (on track for late 2027), AlUla, Saudi Cup, FIFA World Cup 2034 prep, 122M visitors in 2025.
2. **A Thriving Economy** — non-oil GDP, SME growth, employment, FDI, financial markets. 2026 anchors: Tadawul IPO wave, PIF strategy reset, Saudization Phase 2026-2028, SME contribution 22.9%.
3. **An Ambitious Nation** — government effectiveness, e-government, transparency, NGO sector. 2026 anchors: Qiwa platform mandate, ZATCA Phase 2 e-invoicing waves 23/24, SAMA open-banking framework.

### The 13 Vision Realization Programs Almobadir's audience interacts with

These are the operational units of Vision 2030. Almobadir doesn't need to cover all 13, but it should know them by name to map any government announcement to its VRP.

- **Financial Sector Development Program (FSDP)** — owns the SAMA open-banking, fintech, IPO depth story. (Audience overlap: very high.)
- **National Transformation Program (NTP)** — cross-cutting, the operational backbone.
- **Privatization Program** — owns the PIF subsidiary IPO pipeline + new National Privatization Strategy. (Audience overlap: very high.)
- **National Industrial Development & Logistics Program (NIDLP)** — owns the manufacturing, defense localization, logistics story.
- **Quality of Life Program** — owns the entertainment, tourism, sports infrastructure beat.
- **Public Investment Fund Program** — the megaproject + AI infrastructure beat post-April-2026 strategy reset.
- **Hajj & Umrah Program** — pilgrim economy, hospitality, transport.
- **Housing Program** — directly tied to the Riyadh real-estate beat. (Audience overlap: very high.)
- **Human Capability Development Program (HCDP)** — Saudization, skills, female participation.
- **Health Sector Transformation Program** — healthtech, insurance.
- **Doyof Al Rahman Program** — Hajj infrastructure.
- **National Character Enrichment Program** — soft-cultural beat.
- **Saudi Green Initiative / Middle East Green Initiative** — sustainability beat.

When Almobadir tags a piece "رؤية 2030" / "Vision 2030," tagging the relevant VRP secondary (e.g., FSDP, NTP, Privatization) demonstrates fluency the establishment press itself often skips. Source: [Vision 2030 official overview](https://www.vision2030.gov.sa/en/overview).

## Coverage landscape — 5 publishers compared

How Saudi Arabic business media currently treats Vision 2030. The pattern: institutional-grade access, press-release pacing, low founder/creator empathy, almost zero personal-finance translation.

### 1. Al Eqtisadiah (al-eqtisadiah.com)
- **Owner:** Saudi Research and Publishing Company (SRPC) → Saudi Research and Marketing Group (SRMG) — i.e. an arm of the same conglomerate that publishes Asharq Al-Awsat and owns Argaam.
- **Angle:** Establishment business newspaper of record. Government-aligned, ministry-friendly. Daily print + web + Al Eqtisadiah TV channel.
- **Frequency:** Daily, multi-story per pillar.
- **Depth:** High on official numbers, low on independent analysis. Translates ministry briefings into news copy.
- **Vision 2030 framing:** "Progress under Vision 2030" — celebratory, milestone-focused.
- **Source:** [Wikipedia: Al Eqtisadiah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al_Eqtisadiah), [Arab News: launch](https://www.arabnews.com/node/1036351/amp)

### 2. Argaam (argaam.com)
- **Owner:** SRMG (51% acquired Oct 2017 for $10M).
- **Angle:** Tadawul-first financial portal. Real-time market data, earnings, IPO coverage, regulatory filings. Both Arabic (since March 2007) and English (since April 2015).
- **Frequency:** Real-time intra-day; multiple stories per hour during market hours.
- **Depth:** Very high on stock-specific events; lower on macro narrative.
- **Vision 2030 framing:** Mostly indirect — covers Vision through PIF announcements, IPO filings, listed-company strategy.
- **Source:** [Wikipedia: Argaam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argaam)

### 3. Mubasher (mubasher.info)
- **Footprint:** Largest Arabic equity-news platform in MENA. 2.5M+ monthly visitors. Offices in KSA, Kuwait, UAE.
- **Angle:** Investor-first. Stock data + news + tools. Heavier on retail-investor data than feature reporting.
- **Frequency:** Real-time.
- **Depth:** Moderate. Strong on data, light on opinion.
- **Vision 2030 framing:** Stock-impact lens — "what does the announcement do to TASI?"
- **Source:** [Mubasher Saudi](https://english.mubasher.info/countries/SA), [LinkedIn: Mubasher Media](https://www.linkedin.com/company/mubasher-media)

### 4. Asharq Al-Awsat — Business section (aawsat.com)
- **Owner:** SRMG (same family as Argaam + Al Eqtisadiah).
- **Angle:** Pan-Arab broadsheet. Business section runs analytical commentary, expert opinion, regional context.
- **Frequency:** Daily, but business is one of many sections.
- **Depth:** Strong on regional/geopolitical framing; less on Saudi retail-investor concerns.
- **Vision 2030 framing:** Strategic-narrative pieces ("Vision 2030 enters third phase," "energy hub transformation").
- **Source:** [Asharq Al-Awsat business 5266581](https://english.aawsat.com/business/5266581-saudi-vision-2030-enters-third-phase-93-targets-met)

### 5. Mal Magazine (malmag.com.sa) — Saudi business monthly
- **Angle:** Saudi business magazine, longer-form, monthly cadence, executive-interview heavy. Note: web presence less surfaced in this audit pass; primary distribution is print + B2B circulation.
- **Frequency:** Monthly.
- **Depth:** Higher on profile pieces and sector deep-dives than the daily wires.
- **Vision 2030 framing:** Sector-by-sector ("Vision 2030 in tourism," "Vision 2030 in tech") executive narrative.
- **Note for Badr:** Mal Magazine could be a placement target rather than a competitor — they don't compete on the creator/social channel.

### What's missing across all five
- **Creator/founder voice.** None of these are written by, for, or to founders building right now.
- **Personal-finance translation.** Vision 2030 macro numbers rarely get translated into "what does this mean for your job, your savings, your investment portfolio."
- **Arabic-native explainer format** built for Reels / TikTok / Threads, not desktop-first long-read.
- **Post-mortems on failed/scaled-back initiatives.** NEOM scaling back is a massive story; the establishment press treats it gingerly. A creator-native voice can be honest about it without being adversarial.
- **Regulatory-shift navigation for SMBs.** ZATCA waves, Saudization 2026, Qiwa contract mandates, foreign property law — every one of these moves money for an SME owner, and none of these publishers explain them in an actionable way.
- **The SRMG concentration problem.** Three of the five publishers above (Al Eqtisadiah, Argaam, Asharq Al-Awsat) sit inside one media group. That means 60% of the establishment Saudi business voice carries a single editorial center of gravity. Independent creator-voice has a structural permission space that didn't exist a decade ago.

## Recommended editorial line for Almobadir

**Paragraph 1 — Stance.** Almobadir is not a Vision 2030 cheerleader and not a Vision 2030 critic; Almobadir is the **Vision 2030 translator for initiators**. The brand promise across the network ("we share knowledge to empower the new generation of initiators") demands that the single biggest story shaping Saudi business — which the rest of Saudi Arabic media covers institutionally — be retranslated into "what this means for you, your job, your business, your money." The voice should be confident, founder-native, numerate, sourced. It should never read like a press release. It should always end with a "so what for you" beat. When numbers are missed (NEOM, SME 22.9%), Almobadir says so plainly — because the audience already knows, and pretending otherwise costs trust.

**Paragraph 2 — Format hierarchy.** Anchor everything to one of four KPIs: (1) non-oil GDP share, (2) SME contribution to GDP, (3) Saudization/unemployment, (4) Tadawul IPO cadence. Each piece of content names a number, names a source, names a Vision 2030 program (e.g., FSDP, NTP, Quality of Life Program, Privatization VRP), and ends with one specific action the viewer can take. The four sub-handles map cleanly: @almobadir_flousak owns the personal-finance translation (rates, IPO subscriptions, real estate); @almobadir_business owns the SME/regulatory/PIF beats; @almobadir_mindset owns the founder-mindset framing of resilience through the recalibration; @almobadir_media is the agency back-end. The parent @almobadir is the daily macro digest.

**Paragraph 3 — Cadence and surface mix.** Daily Reel/TikTok across the network (one Vision 2030-tagged piece per handle per week minimum). Weekly Threads thread on @almobadir_business. Monthly long-form "Vision 2030 progress digest" newsletter (currently does not exist — biggest gap). Four tentpole moments per year tied to the event calendar: LEAP (Aug-Sep), FII (Oct), Misk Global Forum (Nov), Biban (Q4) — for each, ship a pre-event explainer, live event coverage, and a 7-day post-event recap. The website becomes the searchable reference layer: /vision-2030 hub page, KPI dashboard, regulatory-changes timeline, IPO-tracker. Right now the website has zero visible Vision 2030 surface — that is the single highest-leverage fix.

### Editorial guardrails (the things every Almobadir Vision 2030 piece does)

1. **Name the number, name the source.** Every claim cites a number with a date and a publisher (vision2030.gov.sa, GASTAT, SAMA, Tadawul, Monsha'at, ZATCA, MISA, SPA, IMF). Establishment Saudi press often launders numbers without sources; Almobadir doing so consistently is a trust differentiator.
2. **Name the VRP.** Tag the relevant Vision Realization Program in the caption (FSDP, NTP, Privatization, HCDP, etc.). Trains the audience on the architecture; signals fluency to sponsors.
3. **End with a "so what for you" beat.** Every piece closes on one specific action: "subscribe to this IPO before X date," "register on Qiwa by April 15," "review your e-invoicing setup before June 30." If you can't write the action, kill the piece.
4. **Avoid English jargon-dump.** Arabic-first, with English terms in parentheses only when the English is genuinely the standard term (IPO, KPI, ZATCA, FDI). Do not use English numerals where Arabic numerals fit the context.
5. **Honest on misses.** If a number missed, say "تأخر" (behind), not "في طريقه" (on its way). The audience can tell.
6. **Never quote a minister without naming the source date.** Saudi government statements update fast; a stale quote with no date is a credibility hit.
7. **Run a 30-day correction loop.** When a number is revised (and many Vision 2030 numbers get revised), update the underlying piece and the /vision-2030 hub. Saudi Arabic press largely does not do correction culture; Almobadir adopting it = differentiation.

Five moves above do not all need to land at once. Recommended sequence:
