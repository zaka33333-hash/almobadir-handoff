# Wave 2 Audit — `.cnt3` Content Section

**Audited:** 2026-04-26
**Section:** `<section class="cnt3" id="cnt3-latest">` — "Latest content from the Almobadir network"
**Source:** `website/index.html:833-905`, `website/assets/v3.css:542-602`, `website/assets/v3.js:281-332`
**Heights:** 1143 px @1440 · 1509 px @768 · 1855 px @390
**Screenshots reviewed:** `cnt3-1440.png`, `cnt3-768.png`, `cnt3-390.png`
**Bar:** billion-dollar website. The Verge, Substack, The New Yorker, Bloomberg, Stripe Press as benchmarks.

---

## Executive Summary

The Content section is structurally one of the more competent pieces on the page — the editorial card metaphor is right for an Arabic media network, the accent-color system per sub-brand is meaningful, and the loading skeleton + progressive image reveal is the cleanest motion treatment in the document. **But it is not yet a "billion-dollar" content showcase, and the gap is concentrated in five places:**

1. **A latent layout bug at 1080-1440 px.** The 3-column grid (`1.6fr 1fr 1fr`) gives the featured card `grid-row: span 2` but only ships 2 standard cards. Result: each standard card stretches to fill 2 row tracks and the body padding leaves a visibly empty void below the meta line. The vaunted "featured-vs-standard" hierarchy turns into "tall featured + two awkwardly sparse standards."
2. **Editorial volume is too thin.** Three cards at 1320 px max-width feels like a teaser, not "latest content from the Almobadir network." Bloomberg / Substack / The Verge all show 6-12 cards above the fold. The grand total — 3 cards leading to a single Instagram link — undersells the claimed "+5,000 منشور عبر 6 منصّات" by an order of magnitude.
3. **Accessibility gaps.** No `:focus-visible` on `.cnt3-link` or `.cnt3-cta`. The crimson tag pill on dark glass has marginal contrast (3.4:1 against the cover image — fails WCAG AA for non-text "informational" graphics in some lighting). The skeleton has no `role="progressbar"` and never announces "loading complete." Screen readers hear: "قواعد الأثرياء التسع — اقرأ المقال" (good) but cannot distinguish the featured article from the standards (no role/level cue).
4. **Editorial polish issues.** The `№ 184` glyph is European (numero sign U+2116), feels imported, not idiomatic Arabic. "العدد رقم 184" or "العدد ١٨٤" reads more native. The `≈ ` prefix on read-time is cute but bidi-fragile in mixed Arabic+Latin contexts. The "Compounding" loanword in card-2 excerpt is unjustified (perfect Arabic equivalent exists: "التراكب" or just "تراكم"). The handles use `_` underscores (`@almobadir_flousak`) — when Almobadir is a brand, that's a Twitter-era convention; Instagram convention now is `.` separators (`@almobadir.flousak`) but verify against the actual handles before "fixing."
5. **Photography is generic Unsplash and tonally inconsistent.** The candlestick chart, the handshake, and the dawn-runner each come from a different photographer and a different visual language (saturated-blue chart vs. desaturated-warm handshake vs. cool-blue dawn). For a publication that's about to ask the reader to subscribe ($$ in attention), generic stock erodes editorial authority. The Verge's homepage shoots its own photography or commissions illustration. Stripe Press uses tightly art-directed book covers as the "card art." Almobadir is rendering free stock.

The good: the accent-bar reveal on hover (32→80 px, smooth `--ease-glide`), the 16:9 → 4:3 aspect duality featured-vs-standard, the radial-gradient mouse-follow on the CTA, the staggered reveal (50ms / 180ms / 310ms), and the brand-color tag dots are all detail-grade design moves that **do** belong in the billion-dollar bracket. The structural and editorial issues below are what's keeping this section out of it.

## What works (carry forward)

- The skeleton + image-fade load sequence (`v3.js:286-294`) is the cleanest async UI on the page.
- Per-card `--accent` custom property (set via inline style) propagates to tag dot, accent bar, handle color, progress fill — that single token threading is system-design done right.
- Headlines are real, not lorem. Voice is consistent across all three sub-brands.
- The 16:9 vs 4:3 cover aspect duality reinforces hierarchy without needing more typographic weight.
- Stagger delays (50/180/310 ms) hit the right "settled cascade" rhythm — neither sluggish nor frenetic.
- Section-level radial gradients separate this slab from the void above and below — subtle but felt.
