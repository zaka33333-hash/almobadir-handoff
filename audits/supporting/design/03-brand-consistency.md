# Brand Consistency — Forensic Audit

**Audited:** 2026-04-26
**Source root:** `C:\Users\toner\OneDrive\Desktop\almobadir\website\`
**Scope:** all 9 sections of `index.html`, `tokens.css`, `base.css`, `fonts.css`, `v3.css` (~1,150 lines), `v3.js` (~440 lines).
**Excluded as dead code:** `components.css` (not linked from `index.html`).

---

## Executive Summary

Where the brand is tight: the **token system** is well-conceived. `tokens.css` is genuinely thoughtful — three-tier surface (`void` / `paper` / `ink`), four-axis ink ramp on dark (`ink` → `ink-quiet`), a 4px spacing scale that goes all the way to 200px, dedicated easing curves with intent (`ease-glide` is Apple's Bézier, `ease-snap` is overshoot), three semantic glow shadows tied to brand accents, and aliases (`--bg`, `--fg`, `--line`) that swap on `.slab-light`. The crimson palette is properly variated (3 shades + 2 glows). Spacing in section paddings is disciplined — almost every section uses `clamp()` with `var(--s-*)` tokens. Iconography is broadly consistent: 90%+ of inline SVG uses `currentColor`, `viewBox` is consistent (mostly 24×24 for UI icons, larger for editorial illustrations), and `stroke-linecap="round"` + `stroke-linejoin="round"` is applied wherever appropriate. The `tx-*` typography utility classes in `base.css` are a clean type system on paper.

Where the brand is drifting: **the token system is not enforced**. Beneath the tidy `tokens.css` shell, v3.css does end-runs around it constantly. The Method palette (5 colors that drive a flagship section) is hard-coded twice — at v3.css:398-402 and v3.js:184 — without ever appearing in tokens.css. Two off-token crimson shades (`#FF324A`, `#9C1428`) live inside the hero gradient and SVG. Two off-token "success greens" (`#1a8c4a`, `#34d27a`) appear in the newsletter alone, when the brand already has a perfectly good `--verde: #1FA37A`. **237 hardcoded `font-size: <px>` declarations** and **62 hardcoded `font-weight: <num>` declarations** sit alongside the token scale — every section reinvents its own type ramp instead of reusing `--t-h*` and `--w-*`. Stroke widths drift across SVG icons from 1 to 2.5 — at least 8 distinct values, with no documented "icon weight scale". The fallback font chain is the *resolved* font (since `Mostaqbali`/`Graphik Arabic` 404), and the chains for display vs. body vs. alt resolve to *different* Google Fonts (Almarai vs. Tajawal vs. Cairo) on the same page — meaning at runtime the brand voice is rendered in three flavors of "sort of the same Arabic typeface".

Where the brand is incoherent (concept-level, not just tokens): **the manifesto contradicts the rest of the site**. The hero proclaims `LIVE · شبكة من 6 قنوات · +1.5M متابع` and the meta description literally says "منصة محتوى عربية رائدة". Then the manifesto opens: "نحن لسنا منصةَ محتوى" — *we are not a content platform*. That is a positioning error of the kind that gets a Stripe rebrand thrown out at the first design review. Editorially, the site mixes two voices: a **faux-archival editorial** voice (`§ 001`, `DOC. 001 / EDITORIAL CHARTER`, `DOSSIER · 001`, `FIG. 01`, `REEL · 03`, `EDITORIAL · ٠٤`, `BROADCAST · ٠٥`, `END · DOSSIER 001`) and a **direct-response marketing** voice (`اشترك مجاناً`, `+50,000 مشترك يقرأها كل أحد صباحاً`, `وصلت — تأكيد في بريدك`, `ألغِ متى شئت`). The first voice promises Aperture/The Atlantic/Apollo magazine; the second voice arrives mid-paragraph and reads like a Substack landing page. Either could work alone. Together they cancel.

## Three-paragraph close

The almobadir codebase has bones of a real design system (`tokens.css` is genuinely well-thought-through) but lives a double life. The token layer is treated as documentation; the production CSS treats it as polite suggestion. The result is that almost every quantifiable aspect of the brand — color, spacing, typography, iconography, component vocabulary — has 5-30 variants where there should be 3-10. None of these are catastrophic individually. Together they add up to "this site was made by a small group of designers who didn't have time to compare notes."

The fixes fall into three workstreams that can run in parallel. **(1) Tokenize the runaways**: the Method palette, the off-token crimsons, the success greens, the 32-stop type scale, the 14-stop alpha hairlines — all need to either be promoted into `tokens.css` or rounded down to existing tokens. This is mostly mechanical (sweep replace + linting). **(2) Consolidate components**: one button, one form, one card, one pill, one dot — pick the best implementation among the 3-9 variants of each, kill the rest. This is design judgment work. **(3) Resolve the voice**: the editorial-archival voice is the brand's most distinctive asset, but it conflicts with both the "content platform" claim in meta/SEO and the direct-response marketing copy in the newsletter. Pick one. The faux-archival voice is more defensible (Apollo / The Information / Aperture territory) but requires the marketing copy to grow up to match it.

The single highest-leverage action is **placing the licensed font files in `assets/fonts/`** (or formally adopting the Google Fonts fallbacks as the new primary stack and deleting the dead `@font-face` declarations). Until that lands, the site renders in three different Google Fonts that don't share x-height, terminal style, or weight axis — and *every* typographic finding above (T2-T11) is partially camouflaged by the larger font fidelity gap. Fix the fonts, then run this audit again — at least 8 of the 49 findings will resolve themselves and the rest will become much sharper.
