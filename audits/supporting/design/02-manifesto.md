# Wave 2 — Manifesto Section Audit (`.manif3`)

**Audited:** 2026-04-26
**Source:** `index.html:302-353` · `assets/v3.css:244-274` · `assets/v3.js:114-140`
**Screenshots:** `design-audit/screenshots/manif3-{1440,768,390}.png`
**Bar:** Stripe Press / NYT op-ed / Pentagram-grade editorial polish.

---

## Executive summary

The manifesto is the most **conceptually ambitious** moment on the page — a paper-coloured editorial slab parked between a dark hero and a dark network grid, with a word-by-word literacy reveal driven by scroll position. The intent is right: cream paper + dark ink + crimson accent, mono eyebrow, ornamented caption, "DOC. 001 / EDITORIAL CHARTER." It reads as a deliberate breath, an intermission, a promise.

The execution does not yet earn the ambition. Six issues hold it back from a 10/10:

1. **Arabic ligatures are broken.** Every word is wrapped in `<span class="manif3__word" style="display:inline-block">`. In RTL Arabic this prevents shaping across word boundaries, defeats kashida, and renders each word as an isolated typographic island. This is the single biggest typographic failure on the page and the one most likely to be visible to a Saudi reader before they consciously notice anything else.
2. **`margin-left: .18em` is wrong-axis** for an RTL flow. With `dir="rtl"` the visual gap belongs on `margin-inline-end`, and the hard `inline-block` plus physical-axis margin is what's producing the slightly mechanical, "typed" rhythm visible in the 1440 screenshot.
3. **The brand-display font is not loaded** (Wave 1 finding — Almarai is rendering for Mostaqbali). Every typographic decision below is calibrated against a font the user will never actually see, so issues like letter-spacing −0.01em and line-height 1.25 will need re-tuning once Mostaqbali ships.
4. **The English shadow translation is `aria-hidden="true"`** but is the most legible piece of the section for a non-Arabic reader. Hiding it from assistive tech kills bilingual access for no reason — it's not redundant content, it's the *translation*. SR users get the Arabic only.
5. **Chrome hierarchy is loud** relative to body. The crimson hairline before "§ 001 · المنهج", the spinning ornament, and the bottom-rule + caption divider together create three competing horizontal lines on the bottom third. A real charter document calms down at the bottom; this one accelerates.
6. **The crimson period is doing too much work.** It's positioned after a phrase that already gets crimson + italic + SVG underline + nowrap, so the section stacks four "look here" gestures within one phrase. Stripe Press picks one. So would Pentagram.

The good: the cream-on-cream radial wash is restrained (4% crimson at 85% 10%), the 26ch quote width is correct, the `text-wrap: balance` is correct, the bottom hairline is the right weight, and the eyebrow's `letter-spacing: .18em` is dialled in. The skeleton is sound. The polish is missing.

**Verdict: 6.5 / 10 in current state. Achievable 9.5 / 10 with the fixes below.** Score after Mostaqbali lands and #1–#6 are resolved: 10 / 10. This is one of the two sections most worth the rework (the other being `.mtd3`).

## What works (don't change)

- Cream paper colour `--paper: #F8F4EA` is the right shade — warm enough to read editorial, cool enough not to feel sepia
- The ::before radial gradient at 4% crimson + 5% void is correctly subtle (almost imperceptible — that's the point)
- Eyebrow letter-spacing 0.18em is dialled
- `text-wrap: balance` on the lede is correct (and modern)
- 26ch / 64ch line-length intent is right (the implementation in `ch` is wrong — see #7 — but the design call is right)
- The section sits between two dark sections, creating a real "intermission" rhythm. This is the strongest compositional move on the entire page.
- The bottom-rule transitioning in via `scaleX` from `transform-origin: right center` is subtly RTL-aware. Good.
- Reduced-motion fallback short-circuits cleanly (`v3.js:119`). Good.

## 200-word summary

The manifesto section has the strongest *concept* on the page — a cream-paper editorial intermission between two dark slabs, with an editorial charter framing — but six issues hold it back from a 10/10. Critical: per-word `<span style="display:inline-block">` breaks Arabic ligature shaping; `margin-left` is wrong-axis for RTL; the English shadow is `aria-hidden` so screen readers get only the Arabic; `font-style: italic` synthesises a slant on Arabic glyphs that no native reader accepts; `font-feature-settings: "ss01"` references a stylistic alternate that does not exist in the live (fallback) font; and the brand display font Mostaqbali is 404'd, meaning every typographic decision is calibrated against Almarai. The bottom of the section stacks three competing horizontal gestures (caption divider, ornament row, bottom rule). The crimson is doing too much — accent words, SVG underline, terminal period, eyebrow hairline — Stripe Press would pick one. The word-by-word scroll reveal is gimmicky and unreadable at fast scroll: the user is asked to read while words are still arriving. Six quick wins land in under an hour. Replace scroll-driven reveal with a single intersection-fired fade and the section reads as inevitable. Current: 6.5/10. Achievable: 10/10.
